## Intent

`v0.1.24` exists to give customer-facing builds a practical time limit before the broader enterprise licensing and distribution strategy is complete.

This is deliberately smaller than the full legal/commercial program:

- ship an offline-verifiable entitlement mechanism,
- allow customer evaluation builds to expire after 30 days,
- avoid any license prompt for normal community, education, research, and personal use,
- keep the codebase single-track,
- document that old Apache-2.0 releases remain under Apache-2.0 and cannot be retroactively restricted.

## FEATURE_105: KodaX-AI Fair Core License and Offline Enterprise Entitlement

### Problem

KodaX Space needs to be shared with early enterprise or institutional customers before the full v0.2.x distribution track. Some of those customers are intranet or air-gapped, so online activation is not acceptable. At the same time, community and education users should not be interrupted by a license wizard or forced trial countdown.

Apache-2.0 remains valid for versions already released under Apache-2.0. For future customer-facing packages, KodaX-AI needs a clear path toward commercial rights while the immediate implementation gives customer builds a 30-day timebox.

### Scope Split

`v0.1.24` ships the **customer timebox entitlement MVP**:

- offline signed `.kodax-license` import,
- 30-day evaluation entitlement for customer builds,
- managed-required mode for customer packages or enterprise policy files,
- license status in Settings/About/Diagnostics,
- expiry handling that blocks customer/enterprise-only execution without deleting user data,
- no startup prompt and no countdown for community/permitted free use.

Additional distribution follow-ups remain outside the MVP:

- enterprise purchasing docs and contract templates,
- future SSO/SAML/SCIM or hosted admin surfaces.

Customer-package delivery is not allowed to rely on implicit product copy or the technical entitlement alone. Every customer evaluation package must be delivered with either **KodaX-AI Customer Evaluation Terms**, a signed evaluation order form, or another standalone commercial authorization document that states the 30-day term, non-production/evaluation scope, anti-bypass restrictions, and redistribution limits. Earlier public Apache-2.0 releases remain governed by Apache-2.0 for those released copies.

### License Policy Direction

Use the public name **KodaX-AI Fair Core License** (`KAI-FCL`). The license family starts with `KodaX-AI` so it can apply across KodaX-AI products rather than only KodaX Space.

This is the planned KodaX-AI-owned license family, separate from the technical `.kodax-license` entitlement file:

- `KAI-FCL` is the legal/commercial license artifact: permitted free use, commercial restrictions, redistribution terms, and anti-bypass language.
- `.kodax-license` is the offline signed entitlement artifact: product, edition, license kind, customer, expiry, features, binding claims, and signature.
- `v0.1.24` implements the entitlement mechanism and customer-package enforcement hooks. The later 2026-07-06 distribution update activates the repository-level `KAI-FCL` license switch for releases that include the updated license file.
- Older Apache-2.0 releases remain under Apache-2.0; the new license family only applies prospectively once a future release explicitly ships it.

The intended policy shape is:

- Community edition: free and no-nag for personal learning, individual use, open-source contribution, classroom instruction, and non-production research,
- Professional edition: reserved for a possible future paid organization/pro-user tier; it is not sold, enforced, or used for C-end personal charging in `v0.1.24`,
- Enterprise edition: the current licensed path for company, institution, intranet, managed deployment, and customer-package use,
- school, lab, company, or institution administration, IT operations, team R&D, intranet hosting, managed-device deployment, paid service delivery, or customer redistribution: commercial/managed use,
- enterprise evaluation: signed 30-day evaluation entitlement,
- enterprise production, intranet deployment, managed deployment, commercial redistribution, or paid-service use: commercial license required,
- bypassing or disabling license enforcement for commercial/customer builds: prohibited by the commercial terms.

### Edition, License Kind, and UI Naming

Keep product tier, commercial status, and runtime status separate:

- `edition` is the product lane: `community`, `professional`, or `enterprise`. Community does not require a `.kodax-license` file. Professional is a reserved future lane. Enterprise is the only paid/customer lane used by this MVP.
- `licenseKind` is the business purpose inside a license file: `evaluation`, `commercial`, `partner`, `dev`, or `test`. A 30-day customer file is therefore `edition: "enterprise"` plus `licenseKind: "evaluation"`, not `edition: "trial"`.
- Runtime status should be user-facing and calm: `Community`, `Licensed`, `Expired`, `Invalid`, `Required`, or `Degraded`. Avoid top-level UI labels like `Trial` for the normal product badge.
- All production customer license files must have an `expiresAt` value, including commercial licenses. Longer terms are renewals, not perpetual local secrets.
- The UI label `Licensed to <customer>` means the organization or legal customer named in the entitlement and the accompanying order form/EULA. For community use, do not show a fake customer label.
- The titlebar/version area may show `Community` or `Licensed` next to the version. Detailed fields live in Settings > License and Diagnostics.

### Legal Delivery and Messaging Guardrails

`v0.1.24` is not the full relicensing release, so its legal work is deliberately bounded:

- Customer packages must include a separate evaluation/EULA/order-form artifact. The `.kodax-license` file proves technical entitlement; it is not the full legal contract.
- Public Apache-2.0 releases are not retroactively restricted. Apache-labeled files and packages must not imply commercial-only limits unless the license text for that release actually changes.
- Any release that ships `KAI-FCL` must keep the legal checklist complete before release: copyright ownership audit, third-party contribution audit, CLA/DCO policy decision, dependency/license scan, `NOTICE` review, `LICENSE`/README/package metadata update, and legal approval.
- Public messaging for `KAI-FCL` must use **source-available** or **fair-core** language, not OSI "open source", unless referring specifically to versions that remain Apache-2.0.
- The policy references should stay aligned with the official [Apache License 2.0](https://www.apache.org/licenses/LICENSE-2.0) grant/redistribution model and the [OSI Open Source Definition](https://opensource.org/osd), especially the no-field-of-endeavor restriction.

### Issuer Tooling and Repository Boundary

Yes, F105 needs tooling to produce customer tokens. In product language these should be called **license entitlements** or **license tokens**, not generic access tokens, because they grant offline product rights rather than API access.

Split the tooling into public and private parts:

- **Public in this repo**: entitlement schema, verifier, import/export request code, public-key bundle, fixtures, and tests with non-production test keys.
- **Optional public/dev tool**: a local test issuer that uses a checked-in throwaway dev key and can only create `edition: "dev"` or `edition: "test"` entitlements that production builds reject.
- **Private KodaX-AI issuer repo**: production issuer CLI/service, customer registry adapter, license id generator, renewal workflow, audit log, key metadata policy, and delivery runbooks. Current private project: sibling repo `kodax-ai-license-issuer`.
- **CI/secrets only**: any automated customer-package signing path must read private keys from secret storage; private keys must never be committed, packed, logged, or shipped.

The private issuer project should own production issuance end-to-end:

- issue 30-day `evaluation` entitlements and longer `commercial` entitlements,
- inspect and verify `.kodax-license` files before delivery,
- reject production private-key paths inside the issuer repository,
- write append-only audit records without secrets,
- export only public verification keys, sample requests, and safe fixtures back to KodaX Space,
- keep customer records, production private keys, and production audit logs out of KodaX Space.

Suggested private issuer command shape:

```powershell
kodax-ai-license issue `
  --product kodax-space `
  --customer "Example Corp" `
  --edition enterprise `
  --license-kind evaluation `
  --days 30 `
  --features enterprise-evaluation,offline `
  --binding-request .\example-corp.request.json `
  --out .\ExampleCorp.kodax-license
```

The source-available/package side only needs to verify the resulting `.kodax-license` using embedded public keys. Production token issuance is a business system and should stay outside the public KodaX-Space repository.

### Entitlement Model

Use asymmetric signing:

- KodaX-AI keeps private signing keys outside the client and outside the public repository,
- KodaX Space embeds only public verification keys,
- entitlement payloads carry a `kid` for key rotation,
- signatures should use Ed25519 or another modern asymmetric signature supported by the packaged runtime,
- verification must work fully offline.

Example entitlement payload:

```json
{
  "product": "kodax-space",
  "licenseFamily": "KodaX-AI Fair Core License",
  "licenseId": "lic_xxx",
  "customer": "Example Corp",
  "edition": "enterprise",
  "licenseKind": "evaluation",
  "issuedAt": "2026-06-22T00:00:00Z",
  "expiresAt": "2026-07-22T00:00:00Z",
  "features": ["enterprise-evaluation", "offline"],
  "binding": {
    "mode": "site-or-machine",
    "siteId": "example-corp-intranet",
    "deviceIds": ["sha256:..."]
  }
}
```

Supported MVP modes:

- **Community mode**: no entitlement required, no startup prompt, no trial countdown.
- **Enterprise evaluation mode**: signed 30-day entitlement, fully offline.
- **Enterprise licensed mode**: signed entitlement with expiry and feature claims.
- **Reserved Professional mode**: schema/UI-recognized as a future edition, but not sold or enforced in `v0.1.24`.
- **Managed required mode**: customer installer build metadata or a packaged signed policy manifest sets `license.required=true`; the app opens only the license import/status screen until a valid entitlement is present. Launch flag/env activation is reserved for dev/test.

### Runtime UX

Community use should feel unchanged. The only visible surface is a quiet Settings/About line such as `Community use` with a link to terms.

Customer/managed UX:

- The titlebar/version area shows `Community`, `Licensed`, `Expired`, `Invalid`, `Required`, or `Degraded`.
- Settings > License shows status, customer, edition, expiry, and enabled features.
- Users can import `.kodax-license` by file picker or drag/drop.
- Users can export a request file containing app version, platform, machine id, and requested entitlement mode.
- Expiry blocks new customer-gated runs/actions but keeps project data readable.
- Invalid signatures, wrong product, expired entitlement, and binding mismatch show clear non-secret errors.

### Gate API

F105 should expose a small internal primitive:

- `license.getStatus()` returns `community`, `licensed`, `expired`, `invalid`, `required`, or `degraded`,
- `license.importEntitlement(file)` stores and verifies a signed entitlement,
- `license.exportRequest()` produces an offline request file,
- `license.requireEntitlement(reason)` enforces managed-required/customer-gated behavior,
- `license.hasFeature(featureId)` supports later F098/F101 enterprise gates.

The first implementation should gate only customer-package behavior and explicitly customer/enterprise-labeled surfaces. Personal provider settings, local sessions, and community workflows remain available outside managed-required mode.

### Storage and Tamper Boundary

Store the imported entitlement in app data and mirror it to OS keychain when available. The entitlement is not a secret; authenticity comes from the signature. Do not store private signing keys in the repo, package, or user machine.

Offline enforcement is not perfect DRM. It should catch invalid signatures, expiry, product mismatch, binding mismatch, and obvious clock rollback signals. The business goal is enforceable commercial terms plus reasonable friction for customer builds, not an impossible-to-patch local client.

Clock rollback handling must persist the latest trusted/observed time as `lastSeenAt` or equivalent metadata. If the system clock moves backward beyond an agreed tolerance, the app enters a suspicious/degraded state: it preserves data access and diagnostics, reports a support-friendly reason, and blocks customer-gated execution until the entitlement is revalidated or an authorized recovery path is used.

### Acceptance

- Clean community install starts without license prompt or countdown.
- Customer evaluation packages are delivered with KodaX-AI Customer Evaluation Terms, a signed evaluation order form, or another standalone commercial authorization that states the 30-day evaluation scope, non-production limitation, anti-bypass restriction, and redistribution limits.
- Managed-required/customer build enables `license.required=true` from build metadata or a packaged signed policy manifest, not from an operator-forgotten runtime flag.
- Managed-required/customer build blocks normal app use until a valid entitlement is imported.
- A signed 30-day customer evaluation entitlement works offline.
- Expired entitlement prevents customer-gated execution while preserving data access.
- Invalid signature, wrong product, expired entitlement, and binding mismatch are rejected.
- Production builds reject dev/test license key ids and dev/test entitlement kinds.
- Clock rollback beyond tolerance records suspicion, enters degraded/customer-gated-blocked status, and has automated test coverage.
- Settings/About/Diagnostics show enough license status for customer support.
- Signing private keys are never packaged into the app or committed to the public repository.
- Existing Apache-2.0 releases are documented as not retroactively revoked.
- KAI-FCL release readiness requires the copyright/CLA/DCO/third-party/NOTICE/legal checklist to stay complete before shipping.
- Public docs use source-available/fair-core wording for `KAI-FCL` and reserve "open source" for Apache-2.0 versions.

### Non-goals

- No online activation server dependency.
- No separate Community/Enterprise source tree.
- No current Professional sale or C-end personal-user charging in this patch.
- No broad enterprise admin platform.
- No SSO/SAML/SCIM.
- No license nagging for permitted community/education/research/personal use.
